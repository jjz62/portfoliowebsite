    • ClubView - A Club Database

    Overview

    Worked alongside a team of developers, designers, and product managers to create a website that centralizes key information for Cornell student organizations to simplify the search and application process for students.

    Role

    Product Designer

    Timeline

    October 2021 - May 2022

    Skills/Tools

    Ideation, Prototyping, Figma

    Problem Space

    Positively Impacting our Community

    It is difficult to navigate the hundreds of clubs at Cornell.

    There are lots of events and deadlines to tediously keep up with. Students will often miss information.

    Students often miss deadlines or find out too late for recruitment.

    Particularly in Cornell's pre-professional culture, club recruitment and maintaining applications to student organizations is often stressful and demanding.

    Some organizations need more visibility.

    Many student organizations are unheard of due to the vast depth of different clubs on campus.

    User Groups

    Who can we help?

    After acknowledging the common problem many students had at Cornell, we narrowed our target users into two distinctly defined user groups.

    Club Owners: Students on the leadership team of a club who would use Clubview as a representative of their organization.

    Club Seekers: Students looking to browse through student organizations.

    The Product

    We decided to make something.

    To address the problems found, Design & Tech Initiative, a Cornell project team, started the Clubview team aiming to improve the user experience of searching for and applying to clubs at Cornell by centralizing key club information into a common web platform.

    1. Students

    Students can have an easier time club-searching using current information for a smoother recruitment process.

    2. Clubs

    Clubs can have a platform that is easy to update and can increase visibility.

    Our team drafted an information hierarchy to combine the features we outlined as most important. We prioritized visibility of the home page which would show a large variety of different clubs to search from by making it our landing page. We also housed a majority of the features under the menu to make them intuitive and all listed in one place for ease of access.

    Ideation

    Visualizing a Club

    From low-fi sketches, I began to conecptualize how individual clubs would be shown on Clubview.

    I decided to seperate between a left and right side to distribute different key components to the page. The right side allows users to scroll through larger pieces of content such as descriptions and images.

    The static left side displays more vital information such as name, links, and other categories so that they won't move and will always be visible.
